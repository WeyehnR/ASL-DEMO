The Word "Bat" in Three Contexts

Bat as an Animal (Noun)

The bat is a nocturnal animal that uses echolocation to navigate in the dark. Unlike birds, a bat has wings made of stretched skin. Many people are afraid of the bat, but bats are essential for pollination and insect control.

Bat as a Tool (Noun)

The baseball player picked up the bat and walked to home plate. A wooden bat feels different from an aluminum bat. The team bought a new bat before the championship game.

Bat as an Action (Verb)

She stepped up to bat in the bottom of the ninth inning. He can bat left-handed or right-handed. The coach told the player to bat more aggressively.

All Three in One Paragraph

While watching a bat fly overhead at the evening game, the batter grabbed a bat and prepared to bat. The crowd did not bat an eye when a bat swooped past the bat rack near the dugout.
